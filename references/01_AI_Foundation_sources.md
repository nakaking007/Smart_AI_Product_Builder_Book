# Chapter 1 Source Record — AI Foundations

Last verified: 2026-07-18

## Primary and authoritative sources

1. Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. “Explanatory Memorandum on the Updated OECD Definition of an AI System.” Published 5 March 2024. <https://oecd.ai/en/ai-publications/explanatory-memorandum-on-the-updated-oecd-definition-of-an-ai-system>
   - Used for the operational definition of an AI system and the distinction between inputs, objectives, inference, outputs, autonomy, and adaptability.

2. Vaswani, Ashish, et al. “Attention Is All You Need.” *Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems*, 2017. <https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762>
   - Used for the historical and conceptual account of the Transformer and attention-based architecture.

3. United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization. “Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence.” Adopted 23 November 2021. <https://www.unesco.org/en/legal-affairs/recommendation-ethics-artificial-intelligence>
   - Used for human-rights, dignity, inclusion, transparency, fairness, oversight, and lifecycle framing.

4. National Institute of Standards and Technology. “AI Risk Management Framework.” Released 26 January 2023; official resource page checked 18 July 2026. <https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework>
   - Used for the voluntary AI risk-management context and ongoing lifecycle approach.

5. National Institute of Standards and Technology. “AI RMF Core.” <https://airc.nist.gov/airmf-resources/airmf/5-sec-core/>
   - Used for the Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage functions.

6. National Institute of Standards and Technology. “AI Risk Management Framework FAQs.” <https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework/ai-risk-management-framework-faqs>
   - Used for the trustworthiness characteristics discussed in the chapter.

7. National Institute of Standards and Technology. *Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework: Generative Artificial Intelligence Profile*. NIST AI 600-1, July 2024. <https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.600-1.pdf>
   - Further reading for generative-AI-specific risk management.

## Foundational research for further reading

8. Brown, Tom B., et al. “Language Models are Few-Shot Learners.” 2020. <https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165>
   - Background on in-context few-shot behavior in scaled autoregressive language models.

9. Kaplan, Jared, et al. “Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models.” 2020. <https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361>
   - Background on empirical relationships among model performance, data, model scale, and computation.

## Editorial notes

- The chapter avoids provider-specific interface instructions and pricing claims.
- Product behavior described in examples is conceptual rather than a claim about a named commercial service.
- Legal and professional requirements vary by jurisdiction; the chapter does not provide legal, medical, or financial advice.
- NIST states that AI RMF 1.0 is being revised. Recheck the official NIST resource page before publication.
